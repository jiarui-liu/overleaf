# ICLR 2026 — International Conference on Learning Representations

## Page Limits
- **Main paper:** 9 pages maximum (including figures and tables)
- **During rebuttal:** 10 pages maximum
- **References:** Unlimited, do not count toward limit
- **Appendix:** Unlimited — reviewers are NOT required to read appendices
- **Ethics Statement:** Up to 1 page, does NOT count toward limit
- **Reproducibility Statement:** Does NOT count toward limit

## Required / Recommended Sections
- Abstract
- Main paper body
- References
- **Ethics Statement** (recommended, up to 1 page) — address human subjects, data practices, bias, privacy, or research integrity concerns
- **Reproducibility Statement** (recommended) — paragraph describing code, proofs, data, hyperparameters
- **LLM Disclosure** (required if applicable) — appendix section describing the precise role of LLMs in ideation or writing

## Formatting
- **Double-blind:** no author names in main text or supplementary
- LaTeX template recommended

## Reviewer Evaluation Criteria (Four Core Questions)

1. **Problem Definition** — Is the research question clearly and precisely stated? Is it well-scoped and worth pursuing?

2. **Motivation and Literature Fit** — Is the approach well-motivated? Are relevant prior works cited and discussed? Is it clear why this approach is better than or different from existing methods?

3. **Evidence Quality** — Do results support the claims? Reviewers look for: claims backed by experiments or theory, correct methodology, ablation studies where appropriate, honest reporting of both successes and failures.

4. **Significance and Impact** — Does the work contribute new knowledge? Does it open new directions? Will others cite and build on this?

## Scoring
- Overall rating: **{0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10}** scale
- Rating correlates most strongly with **contribution**, then **soundness**

## Common Writing-Related Rejection Reasons
- Appendix contains critical results not summarized in main paper
- Insufficient ablations to understand what drives performance
- Claims not backed by rigorous experiments
- Missing discussion of limitations
- No comparison to relevant baselines
- Overly narrow problem scope with limited community impact
- Ethics/societal concerns not addressed when relevant

## High-Quality Example Papers (Review Sets)

Before reviewing a paper targeting ICLR, consult the curated review set JSON files to find **topically similar, highly-rated accepted papers** as reference points.

| File | Use For |
|------|---------|
| `review_sets/ICLR2025_50_high_quality_papers.json` | Find strong examples — papers with avg reviewer score ≥ 8, mostly Oral/Spotlight |
| `review_sets/ICLR2025_50_low_quality_papers.json` | Identify common weaknesses reviewers flag — use as a negative reference |

**How to use:** Scan titles and abstracts for keyword overlap with the paper under review. Select 2–3 topically relevant entries with the highest `review scores`. Fetch them via their `link` (OpenReview URL) and study their structure, writing quality, and experimental design. Also read the `strengths` field to understand what ICLR reviewers specifically praised.

See `01_setup/review_sets_guide.md` for the full lookup workflow.
