# COLM 2026 — Conference on Language Modeling

## Page Limits
- **Main paper:** 9 pages maximum (strict)
- **References:** Unlimited, do not count toward limit
- **Ethics statement:** Up to 1 page, does NOT count toward limit
- **Reproducibility statement:** Up to 1 page, does NOT count toward limit

## Required Sections
- Abstract (must be informative — used for reviewer assignment; a weak abstract hurts)
- Main paper body
- References
- **Ethics statement** (strongly recommended, separate page) — discuss societal implications if applicable
- **Reproducibility statement** (strongly recommended) — cover hyperparameters, software versions, data processing details

## Formatting
- Official **COLM 2026 LaTeX template**
- **Double-blind:** refer to own prior work in third person

## Reviewer Evaluation Criteria (Six Dimensions)

1. **Empiricism, Data, and Evaluation** — Strong experimental design, natural data sources, accurate evaluation metrics that validate claims. Do experiments actually support stated claims?

2. **Technological Impact** — Practical utility through high-quality, well-packaged artifacts (code, models, datasets). Are resources usable by the community?

3. **Ambition and Vision** — Papers making "big ambitious leaps forward." Reviewers reward tackling fundamentally hard problems, even if empirical results are imperfect.

4. **Understanding and Principled Approach** — Research that deepens comprehension through careful analysis, ablations, or principled methodology. Papers that explain *why* things work score well.

5. **Clarity, Honesty, and Trust** — Clear writing, balanced and measured claims, released research materials. Overstating results or cherry-picking baselines is penalized.

6. **Reproducibility and Transparency** — Clear method presentation sufficient to reproduce results; code/data availability encouraged.

## Key Principles
- Weakness in one dimension doesn't automatically mean rejection, but strength in one cannot compensate for significant weakness in another
- Reviewers consider compute limitations — papers should not be rejected solely for not running at scale

## Common Writing-Related Rejection Reasons
- Overclaiming results not supported by experiments
- No ablations or analysis explaining *why* the method works
- Missing comparison to relevant baselines
- Reproducibility statement absent for empirical work
- Ethics implications not discussed for socially relevant work
- Abstract is uninformative
- Claims not clearly supported by evidence
