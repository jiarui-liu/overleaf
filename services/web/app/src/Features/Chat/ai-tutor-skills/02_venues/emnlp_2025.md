# EMNLP 2025 — Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing

## Page Limits
- **Long papers:** 8 pages (9 pages camera-ready)
- **Short papers:** 4 pages (5 pages camera-ready)
- **References:** Unlimited, do not count toward limit
- **Limitations section:** Mandatory, does NOT count toward limit

## Required Sections
- Abstract
- Introduction
- Related Work
- Methodology
- Experiments / Results
- Conclusion
- **Limitations (REQUIRED)** — placed before references
- **Responsible NLP Research Checklist (REQUIRED)** — submitted alongside the paper
- References

## Formatting
- Official **ACL style files** (same as ACL formatting)
- A4 paper, two-column format
- Strict enforcement of paper size, margins, font size
- **Double-blind:** no author names or identifying self-references

## Paper Types Accepted
EMNLP welcomes diverse submission types — traditional research, negative findings, surveys, resource announcements, position papers, reproduction studies. Papers need not follow a single template.

## Reviewer Evaluation Criteria (Three Scores)

1. **Soundness** — Technical validity and methodological rigor. Is the methodology correct? Are results valid? Scores must be justified by the review text. A paper can be sound but not exciting.

2. **Excitement** — Subjective interest and novelty, independent of soundness. Lower excitement is acceptable if the paper makes significant contributions (efficiency, new resources, thorough reproductions).

3. **Overall Assessment** — Composite recommendation balancing soundness, excitement, novelty, and potential impact.

## Key Principles
- A good submission clearly states its contribution, provides supporting evaluation, and is comprehensibly written
- Relevant prior work published 3+ months before submission is considered "prior work" and must be cited

## Common Writing-Related Rejection Reasons
- Missing Limitations section (required)
- Missing Responsible NLP Checklist
- Claims not proportional to evidence
- No comparison to relevant NLP baselines
- Overclaiming novelty without citing existing work
- Formatting violations
