# AAAI 2026 — AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence

## Page Limits
- **Main paper:** 7 pages of technical content
- **References:** Unlimited, do not count toward limit
- **Reproducibility Checklist:** Required, appended after references, does NOT count toward limit
- **Acknowledgments:** Must be **omitted** from review submissions

## Required Sections
- Abstract
- Introduction
- Related Work
- Methodology / Technical Approach
- Experiments / Results
- Analysis and Discussion
- Conclusions
- References
- **Reproducibility Checklist (REQUIRED)** — appended after references
- **Limitations** — strongly recommended; clearly describe limitations in scope and generalizability

## Reproducibility Checklist Contents
The required checklist must address:
- Conceptual outline or pseudocode of methods introduced
- Clear distinction between opinions/hypotheses vs. objective facts/results
- For theoretical work: assumptions, formal claims, proofs
- For dataset-dependent work: dataset motivation, availability, citations
- For experiments: hyperparameter ranges, code availability, compute specs, random seeds, statistical significance

## Formatting
- Official **AAAI-26 author kit** (LaTeX or Word)
- US Letter (8.5" × 11"), two-column format
- Type 1 or TrueType fonts only
- **Double-blind:** acknowledgments must be omitted from review version

## Reviewer Evaluation Criteria

**Research Contribution & Clarity:**
- **Problem articulation** — Is the problem clearly explained and well-motivated?
- **Technical soundness** — Is the approach sufficiently detailed for reproduction?
- **Claim support** — Do empirical results genuinely support the paper's claims?

**Evaluation Quality:**
- **Baseline comparisons** — Are appropriate baselines and state-of-the-art comparisons included?
- **Metrics** — Are well-established and reasonable metrics used?
- **Error analysis** — Does the paper analyze errors made by the proposed approach?

**Presentation Standards:**
- Proper contextualization within related work
- Clear articulation of limitations and scope boundaries
- Professional, depersonalized language

## Common Writing-Related Rejection Reasons
- Missing or incomplete Reproducibility Checklist
- Exceeding 7-page main content limit
- Acknowledgments included in review version (reveals identity)
- Claims not supported by experiments or comparisons
- No discussion of limitations or scope boundaries
- Missing relevant baselines
- No error analysis for empirical work
