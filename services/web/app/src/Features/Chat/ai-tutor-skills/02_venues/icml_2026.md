# ICML 2026 — International Conference on Machine Learning

## Page Limits
- **Main paper:** 8 pages maximum (strictly enforced — exceeding causes automatic rejection)
- **References and appendices:** Unlimited, do not count toward limit
- **Impact statement:** Separate required section, unlimited length, does NOT count toward limit
- **Camera-ready:** One additional page (total 9 pages)

## Required Sections
- Abstract
- Main paper body
- **Impact statement (REQUIRED)** — discuss potential societal consequences, both positive and negative
- References
- Optional appendices (reviewers are NOT required to read appendices — all critical content must be in the main paper)

## Formatting
- Official **ICML 2026 LaTeX style files** (LaTeX only — no other typesetting software)
- **Double-blind:** refer to own prior work in third person; exclude grant numbers and identifying information

## Reviewer Evaluation Criteria (Four Dimensions, each scored 1–4)

1. **Soundness** — Claims well-supported by theory or experiments. Correct proofs, rigorous experimental design. Most critical dimension; unsound papers are rejected regardless of other merits.

2. **Presentation** — Clearly written, well-structured, properly contextualized in related work. Is the contribution accessible?

3. **Significance** — Addresses important problems with potential to influence future research or applications. "If this paper's claims are true, how much does it matter?"

4. **Originality** — New insights, deepened understanding, or novel technique combinations. Incremental improvements score low.

## Overall Recommendation Scale (1–6)
- 6 Strong Accept — Technically flawless, exceptional impact
- 5 Accept — Technically solid, high impact
- 4 Weak Accept — Solid but with some weaknesses
- 3 Weak Reject — Clear merits but weaknesses outweigh
- 2 Reject — Technical flaws or inadequate evaluation
- 1 Strong Reject — Well-known results or incomprehensible

## Common Writing-Related Rejection Reasons
- Exceeding 8-page limit (automatic rejection)
- Missing or weak impact statement
- Claims not supported by rigorous experiments
- No comparison to state-of-the-art baselines
- Appendix contains critical results not summarized in main paper
- Incremental contribution without clearly articulated novelty
